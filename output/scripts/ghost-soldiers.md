# Ghost Soldiers (Raid at Cabanatuan)

[0-3s] HOOK
121 American soldiers snuck 30 miles behind enemy lines to rescue 500 prisoners of war — in one night.

[3-9s] FACT 1
On January 30, 1945, the U.S. Army Rangers launched the Raid at Cabanatuan in the Philippines.

[9-15s] FACT 2
The mission: free Allied POWs who had survived the Bataan Death March and three years of brutal Japanese captivity.

[15-21s] FACT 3
The Rangers crawled on their bellies across an open field for over a mile to avoid being detected by Japanese guards.

[21-27s] FACT 4
They were outnumbered — roughly 8,000 Japanese troops were stationed within striking distance of the camp.

[27-33s] FACT 5
Filipino guerrillas — over 200 of them — set up roadblocks and ambushes on every route the Japanese could use as reinforcements.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
The entire raid took just 30 minutes. The Rangers freed 512 prisoners and killed an estimated 530 Japanese soldiers while losing only 2 of their own men.

[39-45s] FACT 7
Captain Robert Prince, just 26 years old, led the assault team through the front gate while his men opened fire from every direction.

[45-51s] FACT 8
Of the 512 rescued POWs, many weighed under 100 pounds. Some had to be carried out on the backs of Rangers and carabao carts.

[51-57s] FACT 9
The raid is considered the most successful rescue mission in U.S. military history. It was later told in the book and film Ghost Soldiers.

[57-60s] FACT 10 + CTA
512 men got to go home because 121 refused to leave them behind. Who's your hero? Follow for more.
