# Night Witches

[0-3s] HOOK
The Nazis were so terrified of these pilots, they called them the Night Witches — and they were all women.

[3-9s] FACT 1
In 1942, the Soviet Union formed the 588th Night Bomber Regiment — the first all-female combat aviation unit in history.

[9-15s] FACT 2
Their mission: fly bombing raids against German positions every single night on the Eastern Front.

[15-21s] FACT 3
They flew Polikarpov Po-2 biplanes — tiny wood-and-canvas crop dusters from the 1920s with no radar, no guns, and no parachutes.

[21-27s] FACT 4
German pilots couldn't track them because the Po-2 flew slower than the stall speed of Nazi fighter planes.

[27-33s] FACT 5
Each pilot flew up to 18 missions per night. The regiment flew over 30,000 sorties during the war.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
They would cut their engines and glide silently over German camps in total darkness. The only sound was the whoosh of wind through their wings — that's why the Germans called them Nachthexen, Night Witches.

[39-45s] FACT 7
Commander Marina Raskova, known as the Soviet Amelia Earhart, personally convinced Stalin to create the regiment.

[45-51s] FACT 8
23 pilots earned the Hero of the Soviet Union medal. The regiment lost 32 pilots over three years of nonstop combat.

[51-57s] FACT 9
The 588th served until the final days of the war, flying their last missions over Berlin in May 1945.

[57-60s] FACT 10 + CTA
Crop dusters versus the Nazi war machine — and the crop dusters won. Could you fly with no parachute? Follow for more.
