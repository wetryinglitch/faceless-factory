# Operation Paperclip

[0-3s] HOOK
After World War II, America secretly hired over 1,600 Nazi scientists — and hid their war crimes.

[3-9s] FACT 1
In 1945, the U.S. launched Operation Paperclip, a classified program to recruit German scientists before the Soviets could.

[9-15s] FACT 2
The mission was to grab every rocket, weapons, and aviation expert from the crumbling Third Reich.

[15-21s] FACT 3
U.S. agents literally paperclipped new fake biographies onto the scientists' files to erase their Nazi party membership.

[21-27s] FACT 4
Many of these scientists had used forced labor from concentration camps. Some were accused of war crimes.

[27-33s] FACT 5
Over 1,600 scientists and their families were brought to America between 1945 and 1959.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
Wernher von Braun, the man NASA celebrated as the father of the American space program, was a former SS officer who used slave labor from Auschwitz sub-camps to build V-2 rockets that killed thousands.

[39-45s] FACT 7
President Truman approved the program but ordered no one with Nazi ties be included — the military simply rewrote their records.

[45-51s] FACT 8
Paperclip scientists contributed to the Saturn V rocket, the Apollo moon landing, and dozens of Cold War weapons systems.

[51-57s] FACT 9
The program stayed classified until 1973, and the full files weren't released until the late 1990s.
