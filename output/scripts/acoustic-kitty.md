# Acoustic Kitty

[0-3s] HOOK
The CIA once spent 20 million dollars turning a house cat into a spy.

[3-9s] FACT 1
In 1961, the CIA launched Project Acoustic Kitty — a Cold War espionage program using a real cat.

[9-15s] FACT 2
The mission: eavesdrop on Soviet officials at embassies by sending a wired-up cat to sit near them.

[15-21s] FACT 3
Surgeons implanted a microphone in the cat's ear canal, a radio transmitter at the base of its skull, and ran a wire antenna through its tail.

[21-27s] FACT 4
The surgery took over an hour, and the cat needed weeks of recovery. One wrong move could've killed it.

[27-33s] FACT 5
The total project cost reached an estimated 20 million dollars in 1960s money — that's over 200 million today.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
On its very first real mission, the cat was released near a Soviet compound in Washington D.C. — and was immediately hit by a taxi and killed.

[39-45s] FACT 7
CIA officer Victor Marchetti later confirmed the project, calling it one of the agency's most bizarre failures.

[45-51s] FACT 8
After five years of development and millions spent, the program produced exactly zero pieces of intelligence.

[51-57s] FACT 9
The CIA officially declared the project a failure in 1967 and classified the files for decades.

[57-60s] FACT 10 + CTA
It's proof that even the CIA can have really, really bad ideas. What would you spy with? Follow for more.
