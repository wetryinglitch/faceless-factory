# Project Pluto

[0-3s] HOOK
"In 1957, America designed a nuclear missile so terrifying, they were afraid to build it."

[3-9s] FACT 1
Project Pluto was a U.S. military program that ran from 1957 to 1964 to build a nuclear-powered cruise missile called SLAM.

[9-15s] FACT 2
The mission: create a missile that could fly around the Earth for months, dropping hydrogen bombs on Soviet cities one by one.

[15-21s] FACT 3
It used an unshielded nuclear ramjet engine — sucking in air, superheating it through a naked reactor, and blasting it out the back at Mach 3.

[21-27s] FACT 4
The exhaust was lethally radioactive. Just flying over a town at low altitude would poison everyone below — even without dropping a single bomb.

[27-33s] FACT 5
It was designed to carry 16 hydrogen bombs and could fly over 100,000 miles without refueling. Ever.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
After delivering all 16 nukes, the missile was programmed to keep flying over enemy territory at treetop level — its radioactive exhaust and sonic boom acting as a final weapon of mass destruction.

[39-45s] FACT 7
The engine, called Tory, was tested by physicist Ted Merkle and his team at a remote Nevada site. It actually worked — they ran it successfully in 1961.

[45-51s] FACT 8
The reactor reached full power in just 5 seconds and generated 513 megawatts of thermal energy during testing.

[51-57s] FACT 9
The Pentagon canceled it in 1964. ICBMs were cheaper, faster, and wouldn't poison half the planet just by existing.

[57-60s] FACT 10 + CTA
