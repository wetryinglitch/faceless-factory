# Tsar Tank

[0-3s] HOOK
Russia once built a tank with wheels taller than a three-story building.

[3-9s] FACT 1
In 1914, engineer Nikolai Lebedenko designed the Tsar Tank — also called the Netopyr, meaning "bat."

[9-15s] FACT 2
Its mission was to roll over German trenches and crush barbed wire like it was nothing.

[15-21s] FACT 3
The front wheels were 27 feet across. That's taller than most houses. It looked like a giant tricycle of death.

[21-27s] FACT 4
The tiny rear wheel — only five feet wide — was its fatal flaw. It carried almost no weight but needed to steer the whole machine.

[27-33s] FACT 5
The entire vehicle weighed 60 tons and was powered by two 240-horsepower Maybach engines stolen from a captured German zeppelin.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
On its very first test drive, the rear wheel got stuck in mud — and nobody could pull it out. The most expensive tank ever built was defeated by a puddle.

[39-45s] FACT 7
Lebedenko personally convinced Tsar Nicholas II to fund it by showing him a tiny wind-up model that rolled across a carpet.

[45-51s] FACT 8
The project burned through the equivalent of millions of rubles before being abandoned after a single test.

[51-57s] FACT 9
The Tsar Tank sat rotting in a Russian forest for eight years until it was finally scrapped for metal in 1923.
