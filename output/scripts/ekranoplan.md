# Ekranoplan

[0-3s] HOOK
"The Soviet Union built a 380-ton flying ship — and the CIA had no idea what it was."

[3-9s] FACT 1
The Ekranoplan was a ground-effect vehicle built by the Soviets starting in 1966 — too big to be a plane, too fast to be a boat.

[9-15s] FACT 2
Its mission: carry troops and anti-ship missiles across the ocean at insane speed while flying too low for radar to detect.

[15-21s] FACT 3
It flew just 5 to 16 feet above the water using ground effect — a cushion of air trapped between its wings and the surface.

[21-27s] FACT 4
One crash during testing in 1980 killed the crew. The machine was so secret, rescue teams weren't told what they were looking for.

[27-33s] FACT 5
The largest one — the KM, nicknamed the "Caspian Sea Monster" — was 302 feet long. Longer than a Boeing 747.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
It had 8 jet engines just for takeoff and could carry 6 nuclear-capable cruise missiles at 340 miles per hour across open ocean — completely invisible to NATO radar.

[39-45s] FACT 7
The genius behind it was Rostislav Alexeyev, a Soviet engineer who envisioned fleets of these replacing the Navy. He died in 1980 before seeing his dream fulfilled.

[45-51s] FACT 8
Only about 4 full-size Ekranoplans were ever completed. The program cost the Soviets an estimated 2 billion rubles over 2 decades.

[51-57s] FACT 9
When the Soviet Union collapsed in 1991, funding dried up. The last Ekranoplan rusted on the shores of the Caspian Sea, where it still sits today.

[57-60s] FACT 10 + CTA
A weapon decades ahead of its time, abandoned by history. Would you fly in one? Follow for more.
