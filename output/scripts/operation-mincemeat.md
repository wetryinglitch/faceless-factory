# Operation Mincemeat

[0-3s] In 1943, a dead man washed ashore in Spain — and changed the entire course of World War II.

[3-9s] Operation Mincemeat was a top-secret British deception plan launched in April 1943 to trick Nazi Germany before the Allied invasion of Sicily.

[9-15s] The goal: convince Hitler that the Allies would invade Greece and Sardinia instead of their real target, Sicily.

[15-21s] British intelligence strapped a briefcase of fake war plans to a corpse dressed as a Royal Marines officer — a man who never existed.

[21-27s] If the Spanish shared the documents with Germany and the Nazis spotted the forgery, thousands of Allied soldiers would sail straight into a trap.

[27-33s] The entire plan rested on one anonymous body — a 34-year-old homeless Welsh man named Glyndwr Michael, who had died from eating rat poison.

[33-39s] Hitler was so convinced by the fake letters that he personally ordered Erwin Rommel and an entire Panzer division transferred to Greece — away from Sicily.

[39-45s] The mastermind was Lieutenant Commander Ewen Montagu, a naval intelligence officer who invented every detail of the dead man's fake identity, down to a photo of a fictional girlfriend.

[45-51s] When the Allies invaded Sicily on July 10, 1943, they faced dramatically weakened defenses — saving an estimated 40,000 Allied casualties.

[51-57s] The operation stayed classified for decades. Glyndwr Michael's true identity wasn't publicly confirmed until 1996 — 53 years after his body washed ashore.
