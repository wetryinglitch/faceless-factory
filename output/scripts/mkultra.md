# MKUltra

[0-3s] HOOK
"The CIA secretly drugged thousands of Americans — and destroyed almost every file to cover it up."

[3-9s] FACT 1
MKUltra was a top-secret CIA mind control program that ran from 1953 to 1973 during the Cold War.

[9-15s] FACT 2
The mission: discover if drugs, hypnosis, and psychological torture could be used to control a person's mind and create the perfect spy.

[15-21s] FACT 3
CIA agents slipped LSD into the drinks of random people — soldiers, prisoners, mental patients, even their own coworkers — without any warning or consent.

[21-27s] FACT 4
At least one person died. Frank Olson, a CIA scientist, fell from a 13th-floor hotel window in 1953 after being secretly dosed with LSD. His family still calls it murder.

[27-33s] FACT 5
The program ran 149 sub-projects across 80 institutions, including universities and hospitals. Stanford, MIT, and Columbia all took CIA money.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
In 1973, CIA Director Richard Helms ordered all MKUltra files destroyed. 20,000 documents only survived because they were misfiled in the wrong building.

[39-45s] FACT 7
Dr. Sidney Gottlieb, nicknamed the "Black Sorcerer," ran the entire program. He personally carried a vial of LSD wherever he went, ready to dose anyone at any time.

[45-51s] FACT 8
The program spent over 25 million dollars across 20 years. They never achieved reliable mind control — but they ruined thousands of lives trying.

[51-57s] FACT 9
It was exposed in 1975 by the Church Committee in Congress. Gottlieb testified but claimed he couldn't remember key details. No one was ever prosecuted.
