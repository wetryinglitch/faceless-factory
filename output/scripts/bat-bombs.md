# Bat Bombs

[0-3s] HOOK
"In 1942, the U.S. military strapped tiny bombs to live bats — and it almost replaced the atomic bomb."

[3-9s] FACT 1
Project X-Ray was a World War Two weapon program approved by President Roosevelt in 1942 to weaponize Mexican free-tailed bats.

[9-15s] FACT 2
The mission: release thousands of bomb-carrying bats over Japanese cities to start massive, uncontrollable fires.

[15-21s] FACT 3
Each bat carried a tiny 17-gram incendiary device with a timer. At dawn, the bats would roost in wooden buildings. Then the timers went off.

[21-27s] FACT 4
During one test at Carlsbad Army Airfield in New Mexico, armed bats escaped and burned down the entire base — hangars, barracks, a general's car. All of it.

[27-33s] FACT 5
A single bomber could release over 1,000 bat-filled canisters, deploying more than 1 million bats in a single raid.

[33-39s] FACT 6 — THE MONEY SHOT
Military scientists concluded bat bombs would start 10 times more fires than a traditional firebomb — and independent tests proved Japanese cities would be virtually defenseless against them.

[39-45s] FACT 7
The whole idea came from a dentist named Lytle Adams, who wrote directly to the White House after Pearl Harbor. Roosevelt read the letter and said "This man is not a nut."

[45-51s] FACT 8
The U.S. spent over 2 million dollars — about 38 million today — on 30 separate test demonstrations over 2 years.

[51-57s] FACT 9
The project was canceled in 1944 because the Manhattan Project was moving faster. The atomic bomb won the race.

[57-60s] FACT 10 + CTA
Bats almost ended World War Two. What's the weirdest weapon you've heard of? Follow for more.
